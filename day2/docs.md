**\*\*** Association in uml **\*\***
Association is a relationship between two classes that indicates that objects of one class are connected to objects
of another class. It represents a "has-a" relationship, where one class contains or uses another class.

In UML (Unified Modeling Language), associations are depicted as a solid line connecting two classes. The line can
be labeled with the name of the association, and it can also include multiplicity indicators to show how many
instances of each class are involved in the relationship.

**\*\*** inheritance association in uml **\*\***
Inheritance, also known as generalization, is a relationship between classes where one class (the subclass or
child class) inherits the properties and behaviors of another class (the superclass or parent class). In UML,
inheritance is represented by a solid line with a closed, unfilled arrowhead pointing from the subclass to
the superclass. This indicates that the subclass is a specialized version of the superclass and can use its attributes and methods.

**\*\*\*** simple association in uml **\*\*\***
A simple association in UML represents a basic relationship between two classes where one class is connected to
another without any specific constraints or multiplicity. It is depicted as a solid line connecting the two
classes, without any additional symbols or labels.
